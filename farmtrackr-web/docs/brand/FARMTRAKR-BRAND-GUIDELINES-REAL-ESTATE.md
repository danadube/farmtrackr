## Logo Guidelines

### Logo Description

The FarmTrakr logo combines house rooflines (representing properties) with layered elements (representing neighborhoods and communities). This creates a distinctive mark that immediately communicates our real estate focus.

### Logo Elements

**House Rooflines**
- Top peaks representing properties and homes
- Color: Meadow Green (#689f38)

**Layered Elements**
- Abstract layers representing neighborhoods and community
- Gradient: Light Sage ‚Üí Forest Green

**Wordmark**
- "FarmTrakr" in rounded, friendly font
- Color: Forest Green (#558b2f) or Deep Forest (#2d5016)

### Logo Variations

1. **Primary Logo (Light Background)**
   - Full color on white or light backgrounds
   - Most common use case

2. **Primary Logo (Dark Background)**
   - Full color on dark backgrounds
   - For headers, hero sections

3. **Primary Logo (Green Background)**
   - White wordmark on green background
   - For branded materials

### Clear Space & Sizing

**Clear Space:**
- Maintain clear space equal to the height of the icon on all sides
- No text, graphics, or other elements within this space

**Minimum Sizes:**
- Digital: 120px wide minimum
- Print: 1 inch wide minimum

### Usage Guidelines

**DO:**
- ‚úì Maintain clear space around logo
- ‚úì Use approved color variations only
- ‚úì Scale proportionally
- ‚úì Place on appropriate backgrounds

**DON'T:**
- ‚úó Stretch or distort the logo
- ‚úó Use unapproved colors
- ‚úó Add effects (shadows, gradients, outlines)
- ‚úó Rotate or angle the logo
- ‚úó Use on busy backgrounds without treatment

## Voice and Tone

### Brand Voice Characteristics

| Characteristic | We Are | We're Not |
|----------------|--------|-----------|
| **Professional** | Knowledgeable, reliable, trustworthy | Stuffy, corporate, formal |
| **Friendly** | Warm, helpful, approachable | Overly casual, silly, unprofessional |
| **Clear** | Direct, simple, easy to understand | Vague, complicated, technical |
| **Confident** | Assured, capable, expert | Arrogant, boastful, pushy |
| **Supportive** | Helpful, encouraging, empowering | Patronizing, condescending, preachy |

### Writing Examples

**Welcome Messages**

‚úì Good: "Welcome back, Sarah! You have 3 new leads and 2 showings today."

‚úó Avoid: "Greetings, user! Your dashboard contains updated data points requiring your attention."

**Feature Descriptions**

‚úì Good: "Track every conversation with your clients. Never miss a follow-up opportunity."

‚úó Avoid: "Our contact management system leverages advanced algorithms to optimize your workflow efficiency."

**Error Messages**

‚úì Good: "We couldn't save your changes. Please check your internet connection and try again."

‚úó Avoid: "ERROR 404: System failure detected. Contact administrator."

**Success Messages**

‚úì Good: "Listing added! We've scheduled your next showing and notified the client."

‚úó Avoid: "Operation completed successfully. Entry has been logged in database."

**Call-to-Action Buttons**

‚úì Good: "Add New Client" | "View Listings" | "Schedule Showing"

‚úó Avoid: "Initialize Client Entry" | "Access Database" | "Commence Scheduling Protocol"

### Writing Guidelines

**DO:**
- ‚úì Use active voice ("Track your clients")
- ‚úì Start with verbs for actions
- ‚úì Use contractions (we're, you'll, they've)
- ‚úì Address users directly ("your listings")
- ‚úì Keep sentences short and scannable
- ‚úì Focus on benefits, not features

**DON'T:**
- ‚úó Use passive voice ("Clients can be tracked")
- ‚úó Use industry jargon or acronyms
- ‚úó Write in third person
- ‚úó Use unnecessarily complex words
- ‚úó Write long paragraphs
- ‚úó Focus only on what the product does

## Design Principles

### 1. Clarity Above All

Every interface element should have a clear purpose. If a feature doesn't help agents manage their properties more effectively, it shouldn't be there. Remove decoration; add value.

### 2. Respect the User's Time

Real estate agents are busy. Design for efficiency. Common tasks should take minimal clicks. Critical information should be visible at a glance. Loading states should reassure, not frustrate.

### 3. Consistent, Not Boring

Use patterns and conventions consistently, but don't sacrifice visual interest. Our color-coding system provides functional variety within a consistent framework. Patterns create familiarity; color creates engagement.

### 4. Mobile-First, Always

Agents work on the go‚Äîat showings, open houses, and client meetings. Design for mobile first, then scale up. Touch targets must be large enough. Text must be readable without zooming. Actions must work with one hand.

### 5. Accessible by Default

Accessibility isn't an add-on. Maintain 4.5:1 contrast ratios. Support keyboard navigation. Provide alt text. Design for screen readers. Everyone deserves great tools.

### 6. Delight in the Details

Smooth animations. Subtle hover effects. Thoughtful micro-interactions. These details don't just look good‚Äîthey communicate that we care about quality and that agents can trust our platform.

### Design System Specifications

| Element | Specification | Notes |
|---------|--------------|-------|
| **Grid System** | 8-point base unit | All spacing in multiples of 8px |
| **Border Radius** | Small: 8px, Medium: 12px, Large: 16px | Consistent rounded corners |
| **Shadows** | Subtle, layered (Apple) or standard (Orchard) | Never harsh |
| **Animation Duration** | 200-300ms | Fast but visible |
| **Animation Easing** | cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1) | Apple-style |
| **Touch Targets** | Minimum 44x44px | iOS guidelines |
| **Max Content Width** | 1400px | Optimal reading width |
